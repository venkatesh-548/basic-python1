Organic Farming
Organic farming, also known as ecological farming or biological farming,is an agricultural system that uses fertilizers of organic origin such as compost manure, green manure, and bone meal and places emphasis on techniques such as crop rotation and companion planting.
Organic farming can be beneficial on biodiversity and environmental protection at local level. However, because organic farming has lower yields compared to conventional farming, additional agricultural land is needed elsewhere in the world, which means that natural land has to be converted into agricultural land. This can cause loss of biodiversity and negative climate effects that outweigh the local environmental gains achieved.
Conventional Farming
Conventional farming is a term that refers to the most common and widely practiced method of agriculture in the world. Conventional are farming uses powerful chemicals to help manage the crop fertility, pests, and diseases. Here are some of the characteristics of conventional farming:
  • Use of chemical fertilisers
  • Use of chemical pesticides
  • Use of chemical weed killers
  • Use of genetically modified
Conventional farming aims to increase crop yields and profits by using intensive inputs and technologies. However, conventional farming also has some negative impacts on the environment, human health, and social justice.
Genetic Farming
Genetic farming is a term that refers to the use of genetic engineering to modify the DNA of plants or animals for agricultural purposes. Genetic farming can have various benefits, such as increasing crop yields, enhancing nutritional quality, improving resistance to pests and diseases, and producing useful substances such as drugs and biofuels. However, genetic farming also poses some risks and challenges, such as ethical concerns, environmental impacts, regulatory issues, and public acceptance
Genetic farming is a controversial topic that has sparked debates among scientists, farmers, consumers, and policymakers. Some people support genetic farming as a way to improve food security, health, and economic development. Others oppose genetic farming as a threat to biodiversity, human health, and social justice. There is no definitive answer to whether genetic farming is good or bad, as it depends on various factors such as the type of modification, the method of production, the intended use, and the potential consequences
Community Farming
Community farming is a term that refers to the practice of growing food collectively by a group of people who share a common interest, goal, or value. Community farming can take various forms, such as community gardens, urban farms, cooperative farms, or community-supported agriculture. Community farming can have many benefits for the farmers, the consumers, and the environment.
Community farming can improve food security and access by providing fresh, healthy, locally-produced food to people who may not have enough resources or opportunities to buy or grow their own food. Community farming can also reduce food waste by distributing surplus produce to those in need.
